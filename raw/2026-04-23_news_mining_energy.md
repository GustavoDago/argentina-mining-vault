# News Mining & Energy Argentina - 2026-04-23

## Vaca Muerta & Energy
- **Pampa Energía:** Requested entry to RIGI for a fertilizer plant in Bahía Blanca. Investment: **USD 2,400 million**. Expected production: 2.1 million tons/year. This project aims to transform the petrochemical sector and leverage Vaca Muerta's gas.
- **Upstream RIGI:** Daniel González (Secretario de Energía) expects 7 upstream projects to apply for RIGI. A recent modification in the profitability ratio (upstream RIGI) aims to extend the productive frontiers of Vaca Muerta.
- **ONs Issuance:** Between October 2025 and April 2026, energy and mining companies issued Obligaciones Negociables (ONs) for **USD 9,900 million**, with USD 6,800 million already liquidated in the local market.
- **Vaca Muerta Sur:** Progress continues at 58%, targeting exports by December 2026.

## Mining & RIGI
- **RIGI Impact:** BCRA reports projects under RIGI have liquidated **USD 1,205 million gross** and **USD 762 million net**.
- **Gualcamayo (San Juan):** Formal approval of RIGI for the "Carbonatos Profundos" project (Resolution 6/2026). Investment: **USD 665 million**. Focus on underground gold mining and a POX plant. Target: 4,500 jobs.
- **M&A Market:** The Argentine market for mergers and acquisitions reached **USD 8,500 million** in 2025 (+63% in value), with energy and natural resources as top sectors. Projections for 2026 remain dynamic.
- **Federalismo Productivo:** AmCham encounter highlights the need to turn macro stability into regional development through mining, energy, and agro.

## Infrastructure & Corredor Bioceánico
- **Corredor Bioceánico Dual:** CAME (Confederación Argentina de la Mediana Empresa) supports the creation of a "dual" corridor in the Tropic of Capricorn to decongest production from Northern Argentina.
- **Integration with Brazil/Chile:** Continued focus on the Capricorn route connecting Brazilian ports to Antofagasta (Chile).

## Regulatory & Macro
- **Ley de Glaciares:** Sector celebrates the reform (sanctioned April 9, 2026) as a boost for legal certainty and federalism.
- **Economy at Two Speeds:** Analysis continues to show a divergence between the dynamic export sectors (mining/energy) and the domestic market.
