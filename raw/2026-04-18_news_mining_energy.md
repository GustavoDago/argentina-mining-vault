# News Roundup - April 18, 2026

## Vaca Muerta & Energy
- **RIGI Expansion:** The national government extended the RIGI (Regime for Large Investments) to the entire upstream oil and gas segment (Decree 105/2026). This is driving projects beyond the traditional Añelo hub.
- **Vaca Muerta Sur Pipeline:** Project progress is at 58%. Operations are targeted to begin in December 2026, aiming for 1.5 million barrels per day by 2031.
- **Asset Transfers:** Neuquén province approved the transfer of Aguada Villanueva, Meseta Buena Espera, and Las Tacanas areas from Pluspetrol to YPF.

## Copper Projects
- **Los Azules (San Juan):** McEwen Copper targets construction start by late 2026. Confirmed investment of US$ 2,670 million. Projected production of 148,000 tons/year starting 2029-2030.
- **Distrito Vicuña / Josemaría (San Juan):** Lundin Mining and BHP plan to start Phase 1 construction (Josemaría focus) in Q1 2027. Budget for 2026 is US$ 800 million. Total district investment estimated at US$ 18,000 million.
- **Taca Taca (Salta):** First Quantum Minerals ratified an initial investment of US$ 4,200 million and signed an ESG agreement with the IFC.
- **El Pachón (San Juan):** Glencore evaluates US$ 9,500 million investment.

## Lithium Sector
- **Catamarca (Río Los Patos):** The Provincial Court of Justice lifted the injunction on the Río Los Patos basin (Salar del Hombre Muerto), recognizing the validity of environmental impact studies. This allows Arcadium and Posco to proceed with expansions.
- **Jujuy (Mesa del Litio):** Jujuy assumed the presidency of the Mesa del Litio. Governor Carlos Sadir launched a "mini-RIGI" for investments starting at US$ 5 million to integrate local SMEs.
- **Posco Argentina:** Confirmed the acquisition of 100% of the Hombre Muerto Norte (HMN) project. Salar de Oro plant operating at 70% capacity.
- **Galan Lithium:** Announced that processing at Hombre Muerto Oeste (HMW) will start in late April 2026.

## Regulatory & Frameworks
- **Ley de Glaciares:** Reform sanctioned on April 9, 2026. Grants provinces more autonomy over water and mineral resources in high-mountain areas. Analyzed as a shift toward local control but potential regulatory fragmentation.
- **RIGI extension:** The regime was extended to July 2027. Current investment announcements exceed US$ 52,000 million.
